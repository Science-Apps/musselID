Lampsilis sietmani Keogh and Simons, 2019

Canary Kingshell

 

Lampsilis sietmani – Holotype, JFBM 22433, [Embarras River south of Charleston, IL]. Photograph from Keogh and Simons (2019), length 127.26 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: thick, moderately inflated.

 

Shell outline: ovate to sub-circular.

 

Posterior ridge: low and rounded.

 

Shell color: olive, yellow, tan, brown or black; surface subglossy to glossy.

 

Rays: may present green or black rays.

 

Shell texture: smooth.

 

Umbo: broad, elevated slightly above the hinge line; umbo cavity shallow.

 

Umbo sculpture: indistinct or discontinuous ridges which are only observed in smaller individuals.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: triangular, thick, erect, 2 in the left, usually divergent but sometimes appear as a single tooth, anterior tooth larger than posterior, 1 tooth in the right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: long, thin, straight or nearly so, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve. 

 

 

Interdentum: long and very narrow or nearly absent.

 

Nacre: white or salmon; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: sexual dimorphism present, but may be subtle and difficult to distinguish, posterior margin rounded to truncate in females, bluntly pointed in males; females tend to be more inflated posteriorly than males.

 

General range

Poorly known but based on extant populations and museum specimens there appear to be two populations, one in east Texas and another in the upper Mississippi River drainage.

 

Habitat

Not well known but based on limited collections occurs in streams to rivers, but not in the headwaters, lakes, reservoirs and canals. In riverine habitats it is often found in nearshore bank and backwater habitats, although occasionally in riffles. Typically occurs in slow to moderate currents, in mud and sand substrates.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
