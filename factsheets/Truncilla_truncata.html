Truncilla truncata Rafinesque, 1820

Deertoe

 

Truncilla truncata – Lake Fork Creek [Sabine River drainage], length 48.6 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: moderately thick and compressed to somewhat inflated; shallow sulcus present anterior to the posterior ridge.

 

Shell outline: triangular to subquadrate.

 

Posterior ridge: well-defined and sharp.

 

Shell color: yellow, yellowish-green, tan, brownish-green, brown, reddish-brown, or black; surface subglossy to dull.

 

Rays: typically presents green or black rays resembling chevrons, zigzags, or blotches.

 

Shell texture: without sculpture.

 

Umbo: broad to narrow, flat to somewhat inflated, elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity shallow to relatively deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: fine ridges that may be concentric or double-looped.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: large, triangular, roughened, 2 divergent teeth in the left valve, 1 tooth in the right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: short to relatively long, straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve. 

 

Interdentum: short, narrow to broad.

 

Nacre: white, occasionally with a pink or salmon tint; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: sexually dimorphic but often difficult to separate, sulcus in females is less distinct.

 

General range

Eastern Texas to northern Alabama, north to Minnesota, Michigan, and Great Lakes basin of southern Canada.

 

Habitat

Streams, rivers, lakes, and reservoirs. In riverine habitats occurs in mainchannel habitats such as riffles or runs with moderate to swift current and in areas with no current such as banks and backwaters. Typically found in sand, gravel, cobble substrates, but sometimes in firm mud or in crevices among large rocks and boulders.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
