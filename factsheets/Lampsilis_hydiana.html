Lampsilis hydiana (Lea, 1838)

Louisiana Fatmucket

 

Lampsilis hydiana – top image, female, Neches River [Neches River drainage], length 65.3 mm; bottom image, male, Neches River [Neches River drainage], length 59.6 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: moderately thin to thick, somewhat to highly inflated.

 

Shell outline: elliptical to oval.

 

Posterior ridge: low and broadly rounded to barely perceptible.

 

Shell color: olive, yellow, tan, brown or black; surface subglossy to glossy.

 

Rays: presents thin or thick rays, which are green or black and remain the same width from dorsal to ventral margin, some individuals may also lack rays.

 

Shell texture: smooth.

 

Umbo: high, broad and elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity moderately deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: absent or consisting of fine double-looped ridges.

Pseudocardinal teeth: slightly compressed to somewhat thick, erect, triangular, and sharp, 2 teeth in left valve, nearly parallel to the hinge line, may be slightly divergent or appear as a single tooth, and anterior tooth larger than posterior, 1 tooth in right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: relatively long, thin to moderately thick, straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve.

 

Interdentum: long, narrow, may be nearly absent.

 

Nacre: white; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: sexual dimorphism present, posterior margin broadly rounded to truncate in females, narrowly rounded to pointed in males; females tend to be more inflated posteriorly than males.

 

General range

Central Texas east into Louisiana west of the Mississippi River and north into southeastern Oklahoma and southern Arkansas.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
