Quadrula apiculata (Say, 1829)

Southern Mapleleaf

 

Quadrula apiculata – East Fork of the Trinity River [Trinity River drainage], length 75 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: moderately thin to thick, somewhat compressed to inflated; shallow sulcus present anterior to the posterior ridge.

 

Shell outline: subquadrate to trapezoidal.

 

Posterior ridge: variable, sharp and narrow near the umbo becoming broadly rounded at the ventral margin, broadly rounded throughout, or almost obscure.

 

Shell color: yellow, greenish-yellow, tan, brown, reddish-brown or black; surface dull to subglossy.

 

Rays: may present narrow to broad green rays.

 

Shell texture: covered with variable sized pustules that may be positioned randomly or arranged in a pattern; pustules are usually present in the sulcus; posterior slope covered with small pustules or low corrugations.

 

Umbo: narrow to broad, somewhat compressed to inflated, elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity moderately shallow to deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: nodulous ridges that blend into the disk sculpture.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: large, triangular, rough, 2 divergent teeth in the left valve, typically with the posterior tooth being smaller than the anterior, 1 tooth in the right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: short to moderately long, somewhat thick, straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve. 

 

Interdentum: short, moderately wide.

 

Nacre: white, iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: not sexually dimorphic.

 

General range

Rio Grande of Texas and Mexico east to Alabama, north to southern Arkansas and Oklahoma, and an introduction in the lower Tennessee River.

 

Habitat

Streams to rivers, lakes, and reservoirs. In riverine habitats found in main-channel habitats such as riffles or runs in sand, gravel, and cobble substrates with moderate to swift currents. Also occurs in nearshore habitats along banks and in backwaters in sand or mud substrates with little to no flow. Can be found in or near brackish water habitats in the lower reaches of coastal streams.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
