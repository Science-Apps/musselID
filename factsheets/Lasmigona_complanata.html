Lasmigona complanata (Barnes, 1823)

White Heelsplitter

 

Lasmigona complanata – Red River [Red River drainage], length 109.4 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: thin in smaller individuals becoming moderately thick in larger individuals, compressed.

 

Shell outline: triangular, obovate or subrhomboid.

 

Posterior ridge: low and distinctly double.

 

Shell color: green, greenish-brown, tan, brown or black; surface dull to subglossy.

 

Rays: may present faint, thin, green or brown rays, which disappear with age.

 

Shell texture: smooth except for irregular, shallow, plications along the posterior slope, which may be absent in some individuals.

 

Umbo: low, broad, even to slightly above the hinge line; umbo cavity shallow.

 

Umbo sculpture: coarse, double-looped or w-shaped ridges.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: triangular, low, thick, and serrated, 2 teeth in left valve, subdivided and extending across the interdentum, 1 tooth in right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: short, poorly developed, may be reduced to rudimentary ridges, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve.

 

 

 

Interdentum: short, moderately wide.

 

Nacre: white, sometimes tinted with a cream-color towards the umbo cavity, may show brassy blotches; iridescent.

 

Other: not sexually dimorphic.

 

General range

Mississippi River basin from northeast Texas to Louisiana north to Minnesota and west to Kansas and South Dakota. Occurs throughout the Ohio, Tennessee and Cumberland River drainages. Also occurs in the Great Lakes basin from Lake Michigan to Lake Erie, the Hudson Bay basin west to Alberta. Within Gulf Coast drainages its distribution is unresolved.

 

Habitat

Streams to rivers, sloughs, lakes, and reservoirs. In riverine habitat typically occurs in nearshore habitats along banks or backwater pools. It is most often found in still to slow current in mud and sand substrates but can occur in gravel substrates with moderate flow.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
