Lampsilis cardium Rafinesque 1820

Plain Pocketbook

 

 

Lampsilis cardium – Lectotype, ANSP 20210, [Ohio River] chosen by Johnson and Baker (1973). Photograph from Graf and Cummings (2019), length 106.0 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: thin in smaller specimens but becoming moderately thick in larger individuals; inflated.

 

Shell outline: subelliptical to subovate.

 

Posterior ridge: broadly rounded to sharp.

 

Shell color: greenish-yellow, tan, greenish-brown, dark brown, or black; surface glossy.

 

Rays: fine, unbroken, green or black rays.

 

Shell texture: smooth.

 

Umbo: high and broad, elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity moderately deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: may consist of coarse v-shaped or double-looped ridges.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: heavy, erect, somewhat compressed, triangular, and serrate, 2 teeth in left valve, nearly parallel to the hinge line, may be slightly divergent or appear as a single tooth, and anterior tooth larger than posterior, 1 tooth in the right valve.

 

 

 

Lateral teeth: short to long, thin to thick, straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve.

 

Interdentum: almost absent or long and narrow.

                                                                                                                 

Nacre: white or bluish-white, occasionally with a pink tint; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: sexually dimorphic such that the posterior margin is broadly rounded or truncated in females, and narrowly rounded to pointed in males.

 

General Range

Mississippi River basin from Louisiana to Minnesota, throughout the Ohio River basin, portions of the Tennessee and Cumberland drainages and parts of the Great Lakes and Hudson Bay basins. Range in Texas uncertain.

 

Habitat

Small streams, but not far into the headwaters, to large rivers. Occurs primarily in nearshore habitats such as banks and backwaters in slow to moderate current in most substrates, but often sand, mud, and gravel.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
