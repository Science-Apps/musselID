Obliquaria reflexa Rafinesque, 1820

Threehorn Wartyback

 

Obliquaria reflexa – Village Creek [Neches River drainage], length 41.0 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: thick, somewhat compressed to inflated; a broad sulcus is usually present between the posterior ridge and row of knobs

 

Shell outline: subtriangular to ovate.

 

Posterior ridge: sharp but may occasionally be rounded; small corrugations often present along the posterior slope, extending from the posterior ridge to the dorsal margin.

 

Shell color: greenish, yellowish-green, tan, brown or black; surface dull to subglossy.

 

Rays: may show fine green or black rays or chevrons.

 

Shell texture: large distinct knobs, up to 4 not including umbo sculpture, aligned down the center of the disk, alternating in position between left and right valves, can be obscure or absent in large individuals; remainder of shell may be covered with small pustules or wrinkles.

 

Umbo: low to rather high, broad, elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity shallow to moderately deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: absent or ridges and knobs.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: triangular, thick, and ragged, 2 teeth in left valve, divergent, 1 tooth in the right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: short, thick, straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve.

 

Interdentum: short and narrow to wide.

 

Nacre: white, sometimes with pink, rose, or purple tint, may show brassy blotches; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: not sexually dimorphic.

 

General range

Mississippi River basin from Louisiana north to Minnesota and west to eastern Kansas and South Dakota. Throughout the Ohio River, Tennessee and Cumberland drainages. Occurs in the Lake Erie drainage. Within Gulf Coast drainages occurs from the Trinity River drainage east to the Mobile River basin.

 

Habitat

Streams to rivers, but typically restricted to large systems, lakes, and reservoirs. In riverine habitat can occur in nearshore habitats such as banks and backwaters with still to slow currents and in mainchannel habitats such as riffles with moderate to swift currents. Occurs in a variety of substrates including mud, sand, or gravel substrates.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
