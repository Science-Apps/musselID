Arcidens confragosus (Say, 1829)

Rock Pocketbook

 

Arcidens confragosus – East Fork of the Trinity River [Trinity River drainage], length 95.0 mm.

 

Other vernacular name(s)

Black Pocket, Black Pocketbook, Rock Shell.

 

Identification

Shell structure: thin to thick, inflated.

 

Shell outline: oval to subrhomboidal.

 

Posterior ridge: rounded or angular near the umbo and becoming rounded near the margin.

 

Shell color: green to brown in smaller individuals, dark green to black in larger individuals; surface dull to subglossy.

 

Rays: absent or subtle green rays.

 

Shell texture: ridges, corrugations and fine wavy ridges near the umbo; sculpturing is typically restricted to the posterior portion of the shell; some populations may only present faint sculpturing; posterior slope may present corrugations and fine wrinkles.    

 

Umbo: high, broad, elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity shallow to moderately deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: irregular V-shaped corrugations extending out in 2 radiating rows on to the shell.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: somewhat large, dorsal-ventrally compressed, serrate, 2 in the left valve, posterior tooth fused with interdental projection forming an arch that extends along the hinge line for a short distance, 1 large, erect, triangular, tooth in the right valve. Lateral teeth:  moderately thick, short, and often indistinct, reduced to a single sharp-edge ridge, usually only 1 in each valve.

 

Interdentum: short to nearly absent.

 

Nacre: white, occasionally with orange or salmon highlights, outer rim of the shell is dark where the periostracum shows through, may show brassy blotches; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: not sexually dimorphic.

 

General range

Mississippi River basin and Gulf Coastal drainages from southcentral Texas to western Alabama.

 

Habitat

Large streams to rivers, lakes, and reservoirs. In riverine habitats primarily occurs along banks and in backwater pools and oxbows in mud, sand, gravel substrates with little to no flow.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
