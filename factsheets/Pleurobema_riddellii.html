Pleurobema riddellii (Lea, 1861)

Louisiana Pigtoe

 

Pleurobema riddellii – Village Creek [Neches River drainage], length 40.0 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: thick and inflated.

 

Shell outline: triangular to subquadrate.

 

Posterior ridge: elevated and sharp throughout, ending at a point near the dorsal margin.

 

Shell color: red-brown, brown, or black; surface dull to subglossy.

 

Rays: none reported.

 

Shell texture: without sculpture.

 

Umbo: high, broad, and elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: absent or curved ridges.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: triangular, thick, erect, and roughened, 2 in left valve, divergent, anterior tooth smaller than posterior and often angled parallel to the hinge line, 1 tooth in the right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: short, thick, and straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve.

 

Interdentum: short and wide.

 

Nacre: white or bluish-white; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: not sexually dimorphic.

 

General Range

Mississippi River basin from Louisiana to southeastern Oklahoma and southwestern Arkansas. Gulf Coast drainages from the San Jacinto River basin to the Pearl River of western Mississippi.

 

Habitat

Small streams to large rivers in slow to moderate currents in substrates of clay, mud, sand, and gravel.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: Under Review

Texas: State Threatened
