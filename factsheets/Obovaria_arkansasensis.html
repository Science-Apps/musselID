Obovaria arkansasensis (Lea, 1862)

Ouachita Creekshell

 

 

Obovaria arkansasensis – top image, female, Neches River [Neches River drainage], length 46.0 mm; bottom image, male, Neches River [Neches River drainage], length 37.0 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: moderately thick and somewhat compressed to inflated.

 

Shell outline: ovate to elliptical.

 

Posterior ridge: low and broadly rounded, almost double in some individuals.

 

Shell color: greenish-brown, brown, or black; surface dull.

 

Rays: may have fine green or brown rays; surface dull.

 

Shell texture: without sculpture.

 

Umbo: low to high, broad, and elevated above hinge line; umbo cavity shallow to moderately deep.

 

Umbo sculpture: absent or faint ridges

.

Pseudocardinal teeth: triangular, thick, erect, and roughened, 2 teeth in left valve, divergent, 1 tooth in the right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: short, thick, straight to slightly curved.

 

Interdentum: short to moderately long, narrow to moderately wide.

 

Nacre: white to bluish-white, sometimes with salmon or purple highlights, may show brassy blotches; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: sexually dimorphic, females truncate posteriorly but more pointed in males.

 

General Range

Mississippi River basin from Louisiana to southeastern Missouri. In Gulf Coast drainages occurs from the Neches River drainage to the Mobile Basin.

 

Habitat

Small streams to medium-sized rivers in mainchannel habitats such as runs and glides or along banks and in pools. Often found in slow to moderate current in substrates of sand, gravel, cobble, and boulders. Can occur in interstitial spaces between rocks, in crevices within bedrock slabs and amongst woody debris.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None

Texas: State Threatened
