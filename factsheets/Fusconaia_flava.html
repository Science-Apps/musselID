Fusconaia flava (Rafinesque, 1820)

Wabash Pigtoe

 

Fusconaia flava – Black Cypress Bayou [Cypress River drainage], length 55.2 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: moderately thin to thick, compressed to inflated; sulcus present anterior to the posterior ridge.

 

Shell outline: subtriangular to elongate-quadrate.

 

Posterior ridge: high, sharp, ends at a blunt point, may show a second or third ridge.

 

Shell color: reddish-brown, yellowish-brown, brown, or black; surface dull to subglossy.

 

Rays: may present green or brown rays.

 

Shell texture: smooth.

 

Umbo: low and narrow in small stream and headwater forms and high and broad in large rivers, elevated above the hinge line; umbo cavity moderately shallow to deep depending on stream type and position.

 

Umbo sculpture: lacking or coarse concentric ridges that form an angle at the posterior ridge.

 

Pseudocardinal teeth: thin to thick, triangular, serrate, 2 teeth in left valve, anterior tooth compressed, parallel to the hinge line and may be separated or joined to the posterior tooth, 1 in right valve.

 

 

Lateral teeth: short to long, thin to thick, straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve.

 

Interdentum: short, broad.

 

Nacre: white, occasionally with pink or salmon highlights, some may show brassy blotches; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: not sexually dimorphic.

 

General range

Mississippi River basin from Louisiana to New York, throughout much of the Ohio River basin, portions of the Tennessee river basin, Trinity-San Jacinto, Sulphur and Cypress drainages of Texas, and Great Lakes and Hudson Bay basins.

 

Habitat

Streams, rivers and some reservoirs. In riverine systems can be found in nearshore habitats such as banks and backwaters but is most common in mainchannel habitats such as riffles and runs. Occurs in mud, sand, gravel, and cobble or a mix thereof in moderate to swift currents.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
