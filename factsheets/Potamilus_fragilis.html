Potamilus fragilis (Rafinesque, 1820)

Fragile Papershell

 

Potamilus fragilis – top image, female, Brazos River [Brazos River drainage], length 110.5 mm; bottom image, male, Trinity River [Trinity River drainage], length 94.0 mm.

 

Identification

Shell structure: thin, brittle, somewhat inflated, particularly near the umbo.

 

Shell outline: triangular, obovate or subrhomboid, may have a well-developed dorsal wing, which can be missing due to erosion or breakage.

 

Posterior ridge: low and broadly rounded to barely perceptible.

 

Shell color: whitish-yellow, yellow, or brown, occasionally stained black in lentic habitats.

 

Rays: may present faint, thin, green or brown rays, which often disappear with age.

 

Shell texture: smooth.

 

Umbo: low, broad, elevated slightly above the hinge line; umbo cavity shallow.

 

Umbo sculpture: absent or faint ridges that may appear double-looped or v-shaped.

Pseudocardinal teeth: subtriangular, low, small, thin, compressed, parallel to the hinge line, 2 teeth in left valve, posterior tooth may be missing, 1 in right valve.

 

Lateral teeth: short to long, thin, straight to slightly curved, 2 in left valve, 1 in right valve.

 

Interdentum: long and very narrow or nearly absent.

 

Nacre: white but can be transparent in smaller individuals. Often with pink or purple highlights near the adductor muscle scars, may show brassy blotches; iridescent posteriorly.

 

Other: sexually dimorphic, in females the posterior margin of the shell is broadly rounded, whereas in males the posterior margin is narrowly rounded.

 

General range
Mississippi River basin from Louisiana north to Minnesota and west to Kansas and South Dakota. Throughout the Ohio, Tennessee and Cumberland drainages. Great Lakes basin from Lake Michigan to the St. Lawrence River system. Within Gulf Coast drainages from at least the Colorado River drainage of Texas to the Mobile River basin.

 

Habitat

Streams to rivers, oxbows, lakes, reservoirs, and canals. In riverine systems found in nearshore and main-channel habitats in still to moderate currents in almost any substrate. Can occur under rocks, in crevices within bedrock slabs and amongst woody debris.

 

Legal listing status

USFWS: None
